The Culturally Engaging Campus Environments (CECE) model, developed by Museus (2013), emphasizes the importance of creating campus environments that affirm students' identities and foster meaningful engagement. At Union Street Center (USC), the CECE framework provides a useful lens for understanding how the physical environment, programming, and staff interactions contribute to residents' sense of belonging and cultural validation.

Culturally Engaging Campus Environments indicators in action at Union Street Center

Staff Practices and Cultural Validation

In my role as Assistant Coordinator for Center Management, a significant part of my work involves supervising 20 Resident Assistants (RAs) and collaborating closely with the Residence Life Coordinator for Student Development (RLC-SD) and the Residence Life Coordinator for Operations (RLC-O). This structure allows for intentional engagement with residents and provides multiple avenues to create culturally validating experiences.

Humanized Educational Environment

Weekly team meetings, one-on-one check-ins with RAs, and feedback sessions with residents serve as opportunities to understand students' cultural backgrounds and personal needs, demonstrating the staff's commitment to creating a humanized, supportive environment (Museus, 2013).

Inclusive Programming and Community Building

USC actively encourages programming that reflects diverse experiences and perspectives. For example, recent events included line dancing nights, movie nights, karaoke, bonfires, and a Neighborhood Block Party. These activities were designed not only for fun but also to foster cross-cultural engagement, collective participation, and community building.

Cross-Cultural Engagement

Residents are encouraged to interact with students from other halls, promoting meaningful cross-cultural experiences that extend beyond USC. Programs are assessed through surveys and feedback, ensuring that residents' voices inform the ongoing design and improvement of community initiatives.

Physical Environment and Cultural Representation

The physical environment of USC complements these practices, though there is room for growth. Spaces such as the Center Desk, lounge areas, and the Union Street Library are accessible and functional, yet the d√©cor remains largely neutral and institutional. While these areas are actively used for meetings, programming, and informal student interactions, incorporating more culturally reflective elements could enhance residents' sense of validation and belonging.

Cultural Representation: According to Museus (2013), when students see themselves represented in their environments, it increases their sense of inclusion and communicates that their identities are valued. Incorporating student artwork, multicultural displays, or posters celebrating diverse identities could enhance residents' sense of validation and belonging.

Holistic Support Systems

Beyond programming and physical space, USC emphasizes holistic support. Residents have access to campus resources through monthly bulletin boards, individual RA check-ins, and connections with graduate assistants and professional staff. This integrated approach addresses academic, social, and emotional needs, reflecting a proactive philosophy that aligns with CECE principles.

Whole Person Recognition

By fostering both personal and cultural engagement, USC helps residents feel recognized as whole individuals, not just members of a housing community.

Challenges and Opportunities for Growth

Engagement Challenges

While USC has made strong strides in creating a culturally engaging environment, challenges remain. Apartment-style housing and the presence of primarily upperclassmen make consistent participation in events difficult. Additionally, opportunities for staff to enhance cultural representation in the physical space could further validate the diverse student population.

Staff Culture: Despite these challenges, the staff culture is supportive, collaborative, and dedicated to fostering connection, making USC a space where students can feel both safe and valued.

Conclusion

Union Street Center exemplifies many aspects of a culturally engaging campus environment through intentional staff practices, inclusive programming, and holistic support systems. Moving forward, enhancing cultural visibility in physical spaces and continuing to innovate programming that reflects the diverse identities of residents will further strengthen USC's alignment with the CECE model and its commitment to student success.
