Physical Environment of USC

Explore how I experience my physical Environment

For the scope of this assessment, my environmental observations are grounded in my role as the Assistant Coordinator for Center Management within Union Street Center (USC). My office is located behind the Cedar Hall Center Desk, alongside my graduate assistant colleague, while other staff - such as the Residence Life Coordinators for Student Development and Operations - occupy adjoining offices within the same suite. In addition to this core workspace, I frequently navigate other physical spaces within USC, including the Lounge Area, Union Street Library, and Union Street Market, each serving unique operational and community functions. My focus throughout this assessment is on how the physical design and spatial organization of these areas shape workflow, collaboration, and engagement among professional, graduate, and student staff. Furthermore, I will examine how the structural layout of USC - within the broader Residence Life and Housing framework - affects accessibility, communication, and the collective functioning of the department as a residential learning environment.
